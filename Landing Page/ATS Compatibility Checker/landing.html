What is an ATS?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software that many employers use to manage the hiring process. It automatically scans and filters resumes based on specific criteria, such as keywords, skills, and experience. This helps employers quickly identify the most qualified candidates and efficiently manage a large volume of applications. However, if your resume isn’t formatted or optimized correctly, it may not get past the ATS and into the hands of a human recruiter.

How do I know if my resume is ATS-friendly?

The best way to know if your resume is ATS-friendly is to check it with an online tool like Internexxus' resume scanner. After you upload your resume and the job description to the scanner, it produces a Match Report. This report highlights the skills and keywords you should try to add to your resume.

How can I improve my resume's ATS compatibility?

To improve your resume's ATS compatibility, use standard fonts and formatting, and avoid headers and footers. Include relevant keywords from the job description naturally throughout your resume. Use clear, simple language and bullet points for readability. Ensure your contact information is straightforward and avoid using tables or images. Keep your resume concise, ideally one to two pages, and save it in a plain text or Word document format to ensure it can be easily parsed by ATS software.
